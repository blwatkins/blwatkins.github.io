Brittni Watkins is an algorithmic artist, software engineer, and educator whose work lives at the intersection of code, creativity, and "What does this button do?".
With a Bachelor of Arts in Creative Computing and a Master of Science in Computer Science from Southern Methodist University, she has spent more than a decade discovering the striking visuals that emerge from logic, geometry, and mathematical form, and crafting the elegant code that brings them to life.

By day, Brittni works as a program specialist and adjunct instructor at SMU's Center of Creative Computation, where she teaches creative coding and builds tools that empower developers and students alike.
Her open-source projects, spanning TypeScript, JavaScript, Node.js, generative art libraries, and production-ready project templates, reflect her belief that well-structured, accessible software is a form of craft in its own right.

As an artist, she channels that same care into her generative art practice.
Brittni began publishing NFT collections on [fx(hash)](https://www.fxhash.xyz/) in 2022, drawn to the medium for its ability to preserve a single output of a random algorithm and for the element of surprise that comes with every mint.
She is a firm believer in the ethos of blockchain technology: decentralization, transparency, and public digital provenance.

At the heart of her practice and her teaching is a simple philosophy: art, art education, and computer science education should be available and accessible to all.
The applications of code are vast and varied; there is space for everyone.

***There is beauty in code, and code can make beautiful things.***
